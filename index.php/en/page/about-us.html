Ziebart Chronology

1953

Kurt Ziebart arrives in Detroit from Germany. He is penniless and has to borrow $18 from his uncle in order to retrieve his bags from the train depot. Soon, Ziebart lands a job as a mechanic at a local automobile dealership. Later that year he is employed in a collision shop owned by Rudy Herman. Herman would later become a partner in Ziebart's rustproofing venture.

1954

While rebuilding a wrecked 1953 Packard in his spare time, Kurt begins his first attempts at rustproofing its body panels.

1959 - 1962

With the sealant perfected, Ziebart and partners Rudy Herman and Joe Selagi (who later sells his interest to George Krausmann) decide to open their first rustproofing shop in the Detroit area. Consumers at first think the product is a hoax, but are soon convinced of its integrity as advertising and word of mouth keep the appointment calendar filled for weeks in advance. To keep up with the demand, 18 franchises offering the service including one in Windsor, Canada, are opened.

1963

Roger Waindle, a recognized expert in the field of corrosion studies, buys the now prosperous business and renames it Ziebart Process Corporation. His plans call for further expansion of the franchise network, refinement of the rustproofing sealant, and securing accounts in the fleet industry.

1964

The famous Ziebart plug is introduced.

1965

Mike Dorey becomes the first International Master Licensee with a franchise in Canada.

1969

Expansion of the Far East begins as a Master License for Japan is granted.

1970

Corporate advertising efforts with Ziebart ads appearing in Life, Look, Time, Newsweek and other national magazines.

1971

Ziebart International Corporation is founded.

1975

Famed actor Rod Serling of the Twilight Zone is hired to appear in national “It's us. Or rust” TV advertising campaign.

1977

Zee-Glaze, a polymer sealant that chemically bonds to paint is introduced.

1980

Ziebart launches aggressive diversification and franchise programs in North America and international markets.

1981

A new contract offering increasing royalties to dealers is introduced in the United States by Ziebart.

1982

Ziebart International Corporation acquires Canada Master Franchise.

1982-1987

Ziebart introduces Rust Eliminator and a three-step paint protection system. Ziebart expands its aftermarket products, services, and accessories.

1988

Tuff-Kote Dinol® franchise system of 130 stores in North America is acquired by Ziebart International Corporation.

1989

Ziebart secures worldwide rights to the Tidy Car® detailing franchise and trademarks.

1990

Ziebart focus groups reveal customer interest in a Ziebart and Tidy Car® business format. Ziebart and Tidy Car® are marketed under a comprehensive business plan.

1991-1993

Ziebart changes look and colors of Ziebart dealer stores; introduces ChipFix®, a paint chip repair system. Company begins focus on vehicle detailing services.

1994

Ziebart International Corporation was bought by its employees through an Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP). This program has fostered personal commitment, dedication and long-term devotion to building the business.

1994

Thomas E. Wolfe becomes President and CEO of Ziebart International Corporation after leading the successful employee-group buyout of the company. Wolfe launches effort to return Ziebart to core services.

1996

Ziebart Japan was sold.

1996

Ziebart sells its manufacturing facilities and Ziebart Products Group subsidiary.

1998

Ziebart aggressively expands franchise sales and introduces new exterior décor, signage program, and showroom merchandising.

1999

Ziebart enters agreement with TCG International to market Speedy Auto Glass® replacement glass and windshield repair services. Ziebart also expands product line to include Rhino Linings® to its product line. The product is the number one selling urethane spray-on bed liner in North America.

2002

Ziebart SuperStores launched within the Ziebart franchise, an over-the-counter store attached to a Ziebart business.

2003

The co-branding and placement of Rhino Linings® and Speedy Glass® within Ziebart stores generates corporate and franchise profits and placement in more than 100 stores.

2006

Ziebart struggles with profitability as many U.S. dealers diversify into non-proprietary Ziebart products.

2007

Ziebart International Corporation sells Canada Master Franchise License to an investment company.

2008

Ziebart simplified the franchising business format to promote a stronger franchise business opportunity and to make dealers more profitable.

2012

Daniel C. Baker, promoted to President of Ziebart Corporation, a wholly owned subsidiary of Ziebart International Corporation. Mr. Baker takes leadership control of U.S. operations for the company.

2012-2013

Ziebart conducts lab and field tests on new Z-Shield® Paint Protection Film to fulfill the corporate mission to have the best paint protection film available in the marketplace.

2013

Ziebart conducts customer focus groups on new proprietary Z-Shield® Paint Protection Film, the most tested, refined, and high-tech product introduced since Ziebart's revolutionary rust protection formula.

2014

The company launches a reintroduction of its brand, the largest such effort in decades, to promote its products, services, and franchise opportunities since the 1970s. - Ziebart introduces Z-Shield®, a proprietary paint protection film, offered in .6 mil and .8 mil film to protect cars from road debris, stone chips, salt, and other daily driving hazards. The new .6 mil film product features 'self-healing' polymer technology that reduces minor scratches and abrasions with heat from sunlight. - Ziebart launches national public relations program to promote the company's new line of products and 55th anniversary since its founding in 1959.

2016

The company officially launched their first U.S. National Charity, partnering with Journey4ACure (J4AC), a Non-Profit, Pediatric Cancer Research Organization. - Ziebart introduced two new products to our lineup, Vehicle Wraps and a new window tint film called, Nano Ceramic Performance (NCP). - Ziebart opens a new master franchise in Bangkok, Thailand.

2019

Ziebart Egypt started operation in early 2019 in Cairo with anticipated locations in Heliopolis and Alexandria. Ziebart also signed the Kuwait franchise agreement.

2020

Ziebart signed franchise agreements in The Netherlands and Australia.

2021

Ziebart expands into the African continent with Ivory Coast and Nigeria, and further into the European markets with Greece.
